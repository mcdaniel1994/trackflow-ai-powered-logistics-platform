# Project Brief

TrackFlow is a last-mile delivery and warehouse management operator serving e-commerce brands in the United States and Spain. The company was founded in 2009 in Los Angeles, operates warehouses in Los Angeles and Zaragoza, employs roughly 130 logistics professionals, and generates about 9M EUR in annual revenue.

TrackFlow stores inventory, picks and packs orders, hands parcels to carrier networks, and manages returns for brands that want logistics handled as a reliable outsourced operation. TrackFlow Tech is the internal unit building the systems, integrations, and intelligent automation needed to replace manual operations with a single cohesive platform.

## Project Objectives

- Build one durable engineering platform across ten engagements instead of isolated demos.
- Replace manual logistics workflows with reliable automation, shared data, and auditable business logic.
- Preserve delivered milestone work while adding forward-looking homes for UIs, services, data pipelines, workflows, and AI agents.
- Give coding agents persistent business and architecture context so future changes respect TrackFlow's constraints.

## Operational Problems

- Los Angeles and Zaragoza run separate warehouse systems with no shared inventory visibility.
- TrackFlow works with eight carriers but has no unified carrier performance data for on-time rate, cost per kg, or incidents.
- Returns represent 18-25% of order volume and are reviewed manually without consistent rules.
- Customer support is entirely human even though roughly 80% of queries are repetitive.
- Commercial teams have no CRM and rely on spreadsheets plus email threads.
- Weekly executive reporting is assembled manually every Sunday night and is already stale by Monday morning.
